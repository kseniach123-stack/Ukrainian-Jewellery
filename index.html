Ukrainian Jewellery

Traditional Beading

While many ancient civilizations influenced Ukrainian jewelry design, the core symbolism originated from pagan Slavic beliefs dating back to the 6th century. You'll find that early Slavic jewelry incorporated symbols of the sun, moon, and natural elements to honor their deities and ward off evil spirits. When Christianity arrived in Ukraine in 988 CE, you'll notice how these pagan symbols merged with Christian imagery. The tree of life became intertwined with the cross, while geometric patterns representing the harvest adapted to form religious motifs. Today, you can see this fusion in traditional pieces like gerdany (beaded necklaces) and dukachi (coin pendants).
